## 0. The three moves, and the two structural facts

Fix a prime p. All objects live on coefficient boxes of monic polynomials over ℤ_p (or
their development-coordinate images); "digit" = coefficientwise p-adic digit; a
**cylinder** is a finite set of digit constraints; nominal volume = the product of the
constrained digits' p-powers.

The OM classifier's read-history reaches every coordinate system it uses by composing
three move species:

- **(M1) development** at a monic key φ (any degree d ≥ 1): f ↦ its φ-adic development
  coefficient vector (a_{ℓ−1}, …, a₀), deg aᵢ < d.
- **(M2) key recentering** (degree-preserving): φ' = φ − p^M·ĉ (deg ĉ < d), and the
  coordinate change = re-development: Θ = M1(φ') ∘ M1(φ)⁻¹.
- **(M3) deg-1-key shift**: x ↦ x + ĉ·p^M on the plain coefficient vector (the deg-1
  instance of M2, listed separately because the residual bookkeeping is the plain Newton
  polygon and the empirical D3/D5 configurations live here).

**Structural Fact A (unitriangularity of division — M1 is always a bijection).** For any
monic φ of degree d and any degree bound, the division f = qφ + r computes the
coefficients of q and r top-down: each is f's corresponding coefficient plus a
ℤ[coeffs(φ)]-combination of STRICTLY EARLIER (higher) coefficients of f (the division
algorithm's elimination step has leading multiplier 1 because φ is monic). Iterating
divisions, the full development map M1(φ) is an affine map of coefficient space whose
linear part is block-lower-unitriangular with unit diagonal. Hence: a bijection of ℤ_p^n
and of (ℤ/p^N)^n for every N, commuting with reduction, and preserving every cylinder
count exactly. *(This is the general-degree D1-marginal; the deg-2, n = 4 instance is
Lemma D1m of `WILD_WAVE8_BLUEPRINT_2026-07-23.md` §1, machine-checked and formalized in
`OM/DevBijection.lean`.)*

**Structural Fact B (bijectivity of every move is free).** M2 and M3 are compositions
M1(φ') ∘ M1(φ)⁻¹ of Fact-A bijections, hence themselves affine unitriangular bijections
commuting with reduction — NO explicit formulas are needed for bijectivity. (The explicit
deg-2 formulas of `JOINT_D1_2026-07-23.md` §1 remain valuable for the Lean layer and for
the ≡ id mod p^M refinement, which for general moves follows from φ' ≡ φ mod p^M:
the two developments agree mod p^M, so Θ ≡ id mod p^M.)

What is NOT free — the mathematical content of each move — is the **landing statement**:
that the move maps its descend stratum ONTO the child cluster box. Sections 1–2 prove a
single lemma from which every landing statement so far probed follows.

## 1. The residual-shift lemma (the one landing computation)

**Setting.** A read at key φ (order 1 in φ; the plain polygon for d = 1, the φ-adic
polygon for d ≥ 2). Fix a side S of integer slope −M and lattice width w, with residual
polynomial R_S(y) ∈ F_Q[y] (Q = p^{d·(residue step)}), deg R_S = w, leading coefficient
= the digit at S's right endpoint (nonzero by the polygon convention). The digits of the
development coordinates ALONG S (at the heights on the line of S) are exactly the
coefficients of R_S.

**Lemma RS.** Let ĉ be a lift of c̃ ∈ F_Qˣ (deg ĉ < d, minimal-support). The move
φ ↦ φ − p^M·ĉ (M2; for d = 1 the shift x ↦ x + ĉp^M, M3, with the sign as the
recentering convention demands — the two conventions differ by the sign of ĉ and both
are covered) transforms the side residual by the variable shift:

  R'_S(y) = R_S(y + c̃)     (in F_Q[y], same side line, same leading coefficient).

*Proof.* Write the development coordinates as the graded pieces along the side: the
height-(line(i)) digit of the i-th coordinate is the y^i-coefficient of R_S. The move
adds p^M·ĉ to the key; substituting φ = φ' + p^Mĉ into the development Σ aᵢφ^i and
re-expanding by the binomial theorem, the term aᵢφ^i contributes to the (j ≤ i)-th slots
with coefficient C(i,j)·aᵢ·(p^Mĉ)^{i−j} plus reduction corrections. At the residue level
of the side line, the reduction corrections vanish (each reduction by the monic φ'
replaces a top coefficient by strictly-lower-height data: it moves mass strictly above
the side line — the same "invisible below the line" accounting as `JOINT_D1` Lemma J1b,
now stated once and for all: a single division step changes coordinates at height h only
through inputs of height ≥ h, with equality only via the unit leading coefficient), and
the digit of the j-th slot on the line becomes Σ_{i ≥ j} C(i,j)·(digit of aᵢ on the
line)·c̃^{i−j} — which is precisely the y^j-coefficient of R_S(y + c̃). For d = 1 the
same computation is the literal Taylor expansion of the coefficient vector under
x ↦ x + ĉp^M restricted to the side line. ∎

**Corollary RS-landing (every descend lands in its child box, with deterministic
transport).** Suppose the stratum imposes: the side-S digit data equals a residual of
the form R_S(y) = (y − c̃)^μ · h(y) with h(c̃) ≠ 0 (a descend at center c̃ of
multiplicity μ; the probed cases have μ = 2), plus exactness of the endpoint digits.
Then after the move:

  R'_S(y) = y^μ · h(y + c̃),  h(c̃) ≠ 0,

i.e. (i) the child's side digits vanish in the slots y^0, …, y^{μ−1} — the child
valuations rise into the multiplicity-μ cluster box exactly as far as the descend
multiplicity forces and no further (the y^μ-slot digit is h(c̃) ≠ 0: the child VERTEX is
pinned); (ii) the co-factor data (the flank sides above/right of S, and h's higher
coefficients) is transported DETERMINISTICALLY — h(y + c̃)'s coefficients are fixed
F_Q-polynomials in the parent digits, and everything strictly off the side line is
untouched at its height (the "invisible" accounting of the proof). ∎

  #{ f mod p^N in the joint stratum with leaf condition C } = #C

*Proof.* Exactly the induction of `JOINT_D1_2026-07-23.md` §4 (accepted), with "J1-step"

### A.1 The side filtration and its graded ring

F_p[x]/(φ̄). Fix an integer slope parameter M ≥ 1. On A := ℤ_p[x] define, for f with

  w(f) := min_i ( v(aᵢ) + i·M ),   v = the Gauss valuation (min coefficient v_p).

**Lemma A1 (the graded ring).** gr A ≅ F_Q[π, y] as graded F_Q-algebras, where π := the

deeper by M. Iteration terminates (degree drops).
(ii) The map F_Q[π, y] → gr A (π, y as above; F_Q = the degree-0 classes) is a graded
ring homomorphism: degree-0 multiplicativity because ρ is a RING hom (reduction mod the

vanish in the graded product. THIS is the honest form of rev-1's demolished "corrections
